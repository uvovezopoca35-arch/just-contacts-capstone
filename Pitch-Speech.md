# Just Contacts — Pitch Speech

**Format:** 5–6 minute pitch + 2–3 minute Q&A
**Slides:** `Pitch-Presentation.html` (7 slides)
**Tip:** Don't read this word-for-word. Rehearse it a few times, then speak naturally. Pause at each "//".

---

## Slide 1 — Title  *(~30 sec)*

> Good morning, everyone. My name is Nikita, and thank you for being here. //
> Let me ask you something simple. How many people have you met this year — at university, at events, online? // Probably a lot. // And how many of them do you actually remember? // The name, the company, what you talked about? // Usually, almost none. //
> Today I want to introduce my project. It's called **Just Contacts** — a personal AI app with one promise: *contacts that actually work.*

---

## Slide 2 — The Problem  *(~1 min)*

> So let's start with the problem. // We meet people all the time, and then we forget them. //
> Business cards get lost in a drawer. // Names slip away after a week. // And the people who could really matter to us — we just lose touch with them. //
> You might say: "But I already have a contacts app on my phone." // True. // But a normal contacts app only stores a phone number. // It remembers the *number*, but it remembers nothing about the *person* — not where you met, not what they do, not what you promised to send them. //
> So the information is there, but it's dead. And that's the problem I wanted to solve.

---

## Slide 3 — The Solution  *(~1 min)*

> My solution is **Just Contacts** — think of it as a *second memory for your people.* //
> Here's the idea. // You don't fill in long forms. // You just write — or even say — whatever you remember. // For example: *"Ivan from the conference, loves snowboarding, works at Yandex."* //
> The AI reads that one sentence and turns it into a clean, organised profile — the name, the company, the interests, the tags — all filled in automatically. //
> And then it helps you stay close: it reminds you about the right person at the right time. // So your network doesn't just sit there — it stays alive.

---

## Slide 4 — How It Works  *(~1 min)*

> Now I'll explain how it works — and it's really just three simple steps. //
> **Step one — Capture.** // You type it, you say it out loud, or you take a photo of a business card. // Whatever is easiest in the moment. //
> **Step two — Organise.** // The AI reads your notes and builds a tidy profile for you. // No forms, no boxes to fill — it does the boring part. //
> **Step three — Recall.** // Later, you can search by meaning, read a short AI summary of the person, and get a gentle reminder before you lose touch. //
> Capture, organise, recall. That's the whole app.

---

## Slide 5 — Inside the App  *(~50 sec)*

> Let me quickly show what's actually inside. //
> There's **AI Quick Add** — you write everything you remember, and the app fills the profile. //
> There's **Voice Input** — you just speak, and your words become a new contact. //
> **Smart Search** lets you ask things like *"who works in design?"* and find people by meaning, not just by name. //
> The **AI Dossier** gives you a short summary of your last meetings and the key facts about a person. //
> **Stay-in-Touch** reminds you about people you haven't spoken to in a while. //
> And it even works as a **Telegram bot**, so you can add and find contacts right inside a chat you already use every day.

---

## Slide 6 — Benefits  *(~1 min)*

> So why does this matter? // Let me give you four real benefits. //
> First, it **saves time** — no more typing into boring forms. //
> Second, you **never forget** — not a name, not a detail, not a birthday. //
> Third, and most important, it helps you build **stronger, warmer relationships** — because remembering people is how trust is built. //
> And fourth, it **works where you already are** — inside Telegram — so there's nothing new to learn. //
> In short: it does the remembering, so you can focus on the people.

---

## Slide 7 — Call to Action  *(~30 sec)*

> Let me finish. //
> We all meet amazing people. // The problem was never meeting them — it was *keeping* them. //
> With Just Contacts, you capture a person once, and the AI does the remembering for you. So you **never lose a person again.** //
> That's why I believe this idea could make a real difference in how we stay connected. //
> Thank you for your attention. // I'd be happy to answer any questions.

---

## Q&A — Prepared Answers *(2–3 min)*

**Who is your target user?**
> Mainly students and young professionals — people who meet a lot of new contacts but don't have time for a complicated CRM. Anyone who networks but keeps forgetting people.

**How would it work in practice?**
> You open the app or the Telegram bot, write or say one or two sentences about a person, and the AI builds the profile. Later you search by meaning and get reminders. It takes a few seconds.

**Why do you think people would use it?**
> Because it removes the effort. Other tools ask you to fill in fields — people are lazy about that. Here you just talk naturally, and the AI does the work. Low effort, real value.

**What problems could appear?**
> The AI could read a note wrong, so I let users edit any profile manually. And because it's personal data, privacy matters — so accounts are private and data is stored securely per user.

**How could you test or improve it?**
> I'd start with a small group of students, watch how many contacts they actually save, and ask if the reminders feel useful. Next steps could be calendar sync and group/event tagging.

- Use the signposts already built in: *"Now I'll explain how it works..."*

- Don't read — glance at the slide, then talk.
- End strong on the call-to-action line.
